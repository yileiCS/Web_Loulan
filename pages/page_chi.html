古城概览

楼兰故城遗址位于新疆维吾尔自治区巴音郭楞蒙古自治州若羌县境内罗布泊的西北角，是汉—晋时期（前2世纪—4世纪）古城遗址1。楼兰一名最早见于《史记》，为古代西域三十六国之一，通往丝路南道的要冲。

楼兰故城遗址呈不规则方形，城中有一条西北至东南的古水道斜贯，古水道东北是佛寺区，南岸为官府区，西部是贵族居住区，西南为平民居住区2。建筑多用土坯或木材建造，因此遗址上残存很多建筑木材堆积。

楼兰故城的建筑既具有典型的汉文化特色，也具有突出的本土文化内容，同时它还蕴涵着大量珍贵文物，这些文物承载着丰富的政治、经济、文化信息，对研究中西交通、东西文化交流和我国古代边疆与内地的联系均有着重要价值3。

City Overview

The Loulan Ancient City ruins are located in the northwest corner of Lop Nur in Ruoqiang County, Bayingoleng Mongolian Autonomous Prefecture, Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region, and are the ruins of an ancient city from the Han-Jin period (2nd century BCE to 4th century CE)1. The name Loulan first appeared in the "Records of the Grand Historian," as one of the thirty-six states of the ancient Western Regions and a key hub on the southern route of the Silk Road.

The Loulan Ancient City ruins form an irregular square, with an ancient waterway diagonally crossing from northwest to southeast. The northeast of the waterway is the temple area, the south bank is the government area, the west is the aristocratic residential area, and the southwest is the civilian residential area2. Most buildings were constructed with adobe or wood, so many building wood piles remain at the site.

The architecture of Loulan Ancient City has both typical Han cultural characteristics and distinctive local cultural elements. It also contains numerous precious artifacts that carry rich political, economic, and cultural information, which are of great importance for studying the East-West transportation, cultural exchanges, and the connections between ancient China's frontiers and inland regions3.
