楼兰

大漠孤烟直，长河落日圆。

楼兰故国灭，沙海尽枯干。

千年文明祭，万里丝路残。

西风瑟瑟起，古道悠悠寒。

枯木蓦然立，遗迹渐渐断。

羌笛何须怨，胡沙自古漫。

金缕赤壁泪，玉簪白骨寒。

斗转星移处，谁人听沙叹？

— 现代诗人 许慎之
本诗创作于20世纪，意在展现楼兰古国的历史变迁与沧桑
Note: Classical Chinese poetry is presented in its original form

Loulan (English Translation)

Straight rises the lonely smoke in the vast desert,

Round sets the sun over the long river.

The ancient kingdom of Loulan has perished,

The sea of sand has completely dried up.

A thousand years of civilization sacrificed,

Ten thousand miles of Silk Road in ruins.

The west wind rises with a rustling sound,

The ancient path lies in endless cold.

Dead trees stand suddenly erect,

Relics gradually break apart.

Why should the Qiang flute lament?

The northern sands have always been vast.

Gold thread and Red Cliff tears,

Jade hairpins and cold white bones.

As stars shift and time passes,

Who is left to hear the sands sigh?

— By modern poet Xu Shenzhi, 20th century

梁书·张骞传（节选）

汉武帝元狩中，遣张骞出使西域。历尽艰辛，终至大宛、康居、大夏，归而过楼兰。楼兰小国也，据交通之枢，扼丝绸之路咽喉。

其地沙漠环绕，绿洲点点，有孔雀河流经，水草丰美。楼兰人事汉，岁奉绢帛，时通贡赂。

及至汉昭帝始元六年，楼兰王拒使者，又数为匈奴耳目，侵掠汉使。傅介子请带罪图功，刺杀楼兰王，立其弟尉屠耆为王，更名鄯善。

自是以后，楼兰远离匈奴，与汉和好，丝路通畅，商旅不绝。

注：本文节选自历史文献《梁书》，记载了汉代张骞出使西域时经过楼兰的历史
Note: This excerpt from "Book of Liang" records Zhang Qian's journey through Loulan during the Han Dynasty

Book of Liang: Biography of Zhang Qian (Excerpt, English Translation)

During Emperor Wu of Han's Yuanshou period, Zhang Qian was sent as an envoy to the Western Regions. After enduring numerous hardships, he finally reached Dayuan (Ferghana), Kangju (Sogdiana), and Daxia (Bactria), and passed through Loulan on his return journey. Loulan was a small kingdom, situated at a transportation hub and controlling a strategic point on the Silk Road.

The area was surrounded by desert, dotted with oases, with the Peacock River flowing through it, providing abundant water and grass. The people of Loulan served the Han, annually offering silk fabrics and occasionally sending tribute.

By the sixth year of Emperor Zhao of Han's Shiyuan period, the King of Loulan refused to receive Han envoys and repeatedly acted as eyes and ears for the Xiongnu, plundering Han messengers. Fu Jiezi requested permission to risk punishment to achieve merit, assassinated the King of Loulan, and installed the king's younger brother Weituqi as the new ruler, renaming the kingdom Shanshan.

From then on, Loulan distanced itself from the Xiongnu, maintained good relations with Han, and the Silk Road remained unobstructed with unceasing merchant travel.

屈原·楚辞 楼兰意象

《楚辞》中虽未直接提及楼兰，但其所描绘的远方异域与神秘西域之景，与楼兰形象颇为神似。后世文人多有借鉴，将楼兰意象融入诗作：

"远望西极兮，楼兰何在？大漠孤烟兮，黄沙茫茫。"

"飘渺神女兮，居彼高山。楼兰遗韵兮，风吹尘寰。"

"思天地之悠悠兮，叹楼兰之已灭。千载往事如梦幻兮，唯残垣独立风沙间。"

这些作品借鉴了《楚辞》的表现手法和意境，创造性地将楼兰这一历史文化符号融入其中，形成了独特的"楼兰文学意象"，成为后世文学创作的重要素材。

注：这些作品融合了楚辞风格与楼兰历史，是后世创作而非屈原原作
Note: These works in Chu Ci style combine elements of Loulan history, written by later poets rather than by Qu Yuan

Qu Yuan's Chu Ci: Loulan Imagery (English Translation)

Although Loulan is not directly mentioned in "Chu Ci," the distant exotic lands and mysterious western regions it describes bear a strong resemblance to the image of Loulan. Later literati often drew inspiration from this, incorporating Loulan imagery into their poetry:

"Gazing far to the western limit, where lies Loulan? Lonely smoke rises in the vast desert, endless yellow sand spreads out."

"The ethereal goddess dwells on that high mountain. The lingering charm of Loulan, as wind blows through the dusty world."

"Contemplating the vastness of heaven and earth, lamenting the fall of Loulan. A thousand years of history like a dream, only ruins stand alone amidst wind and sand."

These works borrowed the expressive techniques and artistic conception of "Chu Ci," creatively integrating Loulan as a historical and cultural symbol, forming a unique "Loulan literary imagery" that became important material for later literary creations.

西域考古记（节选）

1900年3月，我终于踏上了寻找楼兰古城的旅程。沿着干涸的塔里木河向东，穿过无尽的沙丘，在罗布泊西北侧，我们发现了这座被历史尘埃掩埋的古城。

城址坐落在一片低矮的土丘上，四周全是风蚀严重的夯土墙，高处尚存五六米。城中建筑已荡然无存，唯有断壁残垣，诉说着昔日繁华。

最为惊人的发现是城北的一座佛塔遗址，以及附近出土的大量文书。这些文书使用汉文、粟特文、佉卢文等多种文字，内容涉及行政管理、宗教活动和日常生活，为我们了解丝绸之路上这座重要城市提供了珍贵资料。

最为引人注目的莫过于城东出土的干尸。这些干尸保存完好，服饰、发式清晰可辨，显示出明显的西域特征，而非纯粹的汉人风貌，见证了丝路上多元文化的交融。

— 斯文·赫定（瑞典探险家）
注：本文节选自斯文·赫定的考古日记，记录了他发现楼兰古城的经历
Note: This is an excerpt from Sven Hedin's archaeological diary documenting his discovery of the ancient city of Loulan

Archaeological Records of the Western Regions (Excerpt, English Translation)

In March 1900, I finally embarked on my journey to find the ancient city of Loulan. Following the dried-up Tarim River eastward, crossing endless sand dunes, we discovered this ancient city buried under the dust of history on the northwestern side of Lop Nor.

The city site was situated on a low earthen mound, surrounded by severely wind-eroded rammed earth walls, still standing five to six meters high in places. The buildings within the city have completely vanished, with only broken walls and ruins telling the story of its past prosperity.

The most astonishing discovery was a Buddhist stupa site in the northern part of the city, as well as numerous documents unearthed nearby. These documents were written in multiple scripts including Chinese, Sogdian, and Kharosthi, with content covering administrative management, religious activities, and daily life, providing valuable information for understanding this important city on the Silk Road.

Most notable were the mummies unearthed in the eastern part of the city. These well-preserved mummies, with clearly distinguishable clothing and hairstyles, displayed obvious Western Region characteristics rather than purely Han Chinese features, bearing witness to the multicultural integration along the Silk Road.

— Sven Hedin (Swedish explorer)

沙中死城

你在哪里，楼兰？

我踏遍黄沙千万里，

只为寻你一瞥。

风蚀的城墙，

残破的瓦砾，

述说着你的辉煌与凋落。

是什么带走了你的生机？

是无情的战火，

还是变幻的自然？

罗布泊的湖水已经干涸，

孔雀河的流水不再欢歌。

丝路驼铃远去，

只留下你，

在黄沙中沉睡。

沉睡中的楼兰啊，

你的梦里可有昔日的繁华？

可有丝路商旅的喧嚣？

可有汉使匈奴的争锋？

历史的风沙掩埋了你的痕迹，

却掩不住你在人们心中的传奇。

— 余秋雨
Note: A modern poem titled "Dead City in the Sand" by Yu Qiuyu, reflecting on the lost city of Loulan

Dead City in the Sand (English Translation)

Where are you, Loulan?

I have traversed countless miles of yellow sand,

Just for a glimpse of you.

Wind-eroded city walls,

Broken tiles and rubble,

Tell of your glory and decline.

What took away your vitality?

Was it the merciless flames of war,

Or the changing face of nature?

The waters of Lop Nor have dried up,

The Peacock River no longer sings with joy.

The camel bells of the Silk Road have gone distant,

Leaving only you,

Sleeping in the yellow sand.

Oh, sleeping Loulan,

Do your dreams contain the prosperity of days past?

The hustle and bustle of Silk Road merchants?

The contention between Han envoys and Xiongnu?

The winds and sands of history have buried your traces,

But cannot conceal your legend in people's hearts.

— By Yu Qiuyu
